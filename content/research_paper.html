--- title : paper --- ### Zerocoin: Anonymous Distributed E-Cash from Bitcoin ####Ian Miers, Christina Garman, Matthew Green, Aviel D. Rubin [IEEE Symposium on Security and Privacy (Oakland) 2013](http://www.ieee-security.org/TC/SP2013/) | [PDF]({{media_url('pdf/ZerocoinOakland.pdf')}})| Abstract: > Bitcoin is the ﬁrst e-cash system to see widespread > adoption. While Bitcoin offers the potential for new types of > ﬁnancial interaction, it has signiﬁcant limitations regarding > privacy. Speciﬁcally, because the Bitcoin transaction log is > completely public, users’ privacy is protected only through > the use of pseudonyms. In this paper we propose Zerocoin, a > cryptographic extension to Bitcoin that augments the protocol > to allow for fully anonymous currency transactions. Our system > uses standard cryptographic assumptions and does not introduce > new trusted parties or otherwise change the security model of > Bitcoin. We detail Zerocoin’s cryptographic construction, its > integration into Bitcoin, and examine its performance both in > terms of computation and impact on the Bitcoin protocol.
